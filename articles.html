Recommended Articles

Fama, Eugene F . My Life in Finance[J]. Annual Review of Financial Economics, 2011, 3(1):1-15.

Eugene F. Fama is Finance professor at the University of Chicago, and Nobel Laureate in Economics, 2013. He is also known as Father of Mondern Finance for this contributions in Efficient Markets,Event Studies, Three-Factor Model. His work in Three-factor model has also been widely cited in emperical research in marketing, known as Marketing-Finance Interface, one the the several mainstreams in preminer marketing journals. This paper is an autobiography to the Annual Review of Financial Economics by Fama.

Philip J. Guo (2012), The Ph.D. Grind.

Philip J. Guo was former PhD Student in Computer Science at Stanford University between 2006 - 2012. This 115 page minibook, is an reflection of a PhD student's 6-year student life in the academic game. which receives popularity among research students worldwide. This minibook is recommended for students who aim at pursuing doctorate degree or who are in the course of the academic game.

Abhishek Nagaraj（2010), A Guide to Business PhD Applications.

The author is originally from India, former PhD student at Sloan School of Management, MIT. This document is meant to serve as a primer to those considering applying to PhD programs offered by US business schools. Main objective will be to help you think through this career option and guide your application process.
